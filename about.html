Your Future Weather, Anytime, Anywhere

Weather forecasting is crucial across various fields, affecting agriculture, disaster preparedness, and transportation. Traditional forecasting methods fall into two main categories theory-driven and data-driven. Theory-driven methods rely on complex atmospheric simulations, often requiring supercomputers, while data-driven methods use historical weather data through deep learning models. However, many data-driven approaches either treat all weather variables as a single dataset, missing detailed station-level correlations, or employ large neural networks with numerous parameters. To overcome these limitations; we propose a spatio-temporal self-supervised approach with XGBoost. This approach uses XGBoost to effectively identify detailed spatial relationships and temporal patterns. By combining the efficiency of XGBoost with spatio-temporal learning techniques, it aims to improve the accuracy of weather forecasts.
